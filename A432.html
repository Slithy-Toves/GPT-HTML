Triangles and Tuning Standards

An article at izotope.com about the purported benefits of tuning to 432 Hz rather than the current standard A440 states that "an equilateral triangle whose area and perimeter are equal has a square root of 432." Below we examine the math behind that statement.

First, we use the Pythagorean theorem, the square upon the hypotenuse of a right triangle is equal to the sum of the squares upon the other two sides, to find the altitude, or height (h), of an equilateral triangle.

\(h^2 + (\frac{S}{2})^2 = s^2\)

\(h^2 = s^2 - (\frac{S}{2})^2\)

\(h^2 = \frac{3}{4}s^2\)

\(h = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}s\)

Next, we find the area of the triangle by using the formula area equals one half base times height.

\(a = \frac{1}{2}s * (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})s\)

\(a = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2\)

Now we set the area of the triangle, \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2\), equal to the perimeter which is s + s + s, or 3s, and then solve for s.

\(3s = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2\)

\(3 = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}s\)

\(s = \frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} * 3 = \frac{12}{\sqrt{3}}\)

Substituting this value of s into the expression for the area we derived above gives us:

a = \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\)\(\left(\frac{12}{\sqrt{3}}\right)^2\) = \(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}\)\(\frac{144}{3}\) = \(12\sqrt{3}\)

(The perimeter is three times the side, or 3 * \(\frac{12}{\sqrt(3)}\) = \(\frac{36}{\sqrt(3)}\) which also equals \(12 \sqrt{3}\) as you can see by multipying \(\frac {\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}\) times \(\frac{36}{\sqrt3}\)).

The next question: is \(12\sqrt{3}\) equal to \(\sqrt{432}\)?

To determine that we factor 432 into \(2*2*2*2*3*3*3\) or \(4^2*3^2*3\) and take the square root.

\(\sqrt{4^2*3^2*3}\) = \((4 * 3) * \sqrt{3}\) which equals \(12 \sqrt{3}\)

So, while the original statement, "an equilateral triangle whose area and perimeter are equal has a square root of 432", is vague and imprecise, it is true that, for an eqilateral triangle which has equal numerical values for both its area and perimeter (the area and perimeter cannot actually be "equal" since one has a unit of length and the other a unit of area) that numerical value is the square root of 432, namely \(12 \sqrt{3}\).
